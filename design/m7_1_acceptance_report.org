* M7.1 Acceptance and Freeze Report

This document records the formal acceptance and version freeze of the M7.1 Classification Subsystem within the ORGA project.

** 1. M7 / M7.1 Objectives

The primary goal of M7 and M7.1 was to advance the classification subsystem from an inactive, highly noisy, "union-based" prototype into a robust, convergent, and institution-level classification engine, without compromising the 100% stability baseline achieved in M6.1b.

** 2. Key Improvements Implemented

1. **Activation of the Taxonomy Engine:** Deployed a foundational `DEFAULT_TAXONOMY` bridging the gap between raw text signals and the `RuleBasedClassifier` (Tier 1).
2. **Page Weighting Architecture:** Implemented dynamic `page_role_weight` (e.g., scoring `/about` significantly higher than `/news`), reducing peripheral noise.
3. **Category Suppression Matrix:** Replaced naive union aggregation with an authoritative `ClassificationAggregator` that actively suppresses competing lower-confidence signals (e.g., a strong "Hospital" signal suppresses weak "Association" noise).
4. **Resilient Microservices:** Maintained 100% batch processing stability across 26 high-complexity global organization targets via extensive defensive null-handling and `try-except` containment blocks.

** 3. Current Engineering Watermark (The Freeze State)

The system is now formally **FROZEN**.

- **Stability:** 100% success rate on the validation batch with a ~0.65s/URL processing speed. No systemic 500 errors or `NoneType` crashes remain.
- **Classification Convergence:** Primary categories are highly accurate. Peripheral noise is effectively mitigated.
- **Explainability:** Full decision trees, score distributions, and suppression rationales are embedded in the response payload's `debug_info`.

** 4. Rationale for the Freeze Decision

The current technology stack (Rule matching + Bayesian Fallback + Aggregation) has reached a point of diminishing returns. The system operates predictably at the absolute boundary of what deterministic rules can achieve.

Any further attempt to arbitrarily force secondary categories out of specific test targets (e.g., removing a "Government" secondary tag from a public hospital) would necessitate breaking the "No Site-Specific Patching" rule, introducing unacceptable fragility and maintenance debt.

The system is now transitioning to **M8: Open Source Release Preparation and Documentation**. Further algorithmic enhancements are deferred to post-release architectural revisions.
